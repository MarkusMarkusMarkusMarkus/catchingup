Oneshotting

“Oneshotting as practice and discovery”

A thing that has been difficult to keep in mind wrt GenAI is just when to use it.

Like, what can models actually do? What can I use them for?

I’m still discovering that. And, because I’m still discovering it, I haven’t yet developed the reflex of using them when appropriate, the way I have a developed reflex of opening Google when I want to search for something or Wikipedia when I want to learn about something else.

To counteract the lack of this reflex I have felt the need to go to an extreme: can I use it for… everything?

Just how much can it do? With how little? Can I just tell it, directly, what I want the way I would say to myself, and will that work? Because one of two things will happen: 1) it’ll actually do whatever I want or 2) it’ll fail to, but I’ll learn what I can do with it.

Here’s an example of a former, where it one-shots (i.e. builds an immediately satisfactory version just from a direct request) a task management system (that I’ve since been using! It’s so pretty!)

You can see that I just asked it directly for what I wanted. And it did that and made it pretty.

Why am I telling you all of this? To imprint the idea that models are — especially now — shockingly capable. And, because they are shockingly capable, you basically will err less if you overestimate them than if you underestimate them.

If you only ask them for what you think is easy stuff for them they won’t ever fail you but they won’t surprise you either. If you ask them for what you think is hard stuff for them… then you give them a chance to surprise you, and yourself a chance to learn their limits.

To start educating my reflexes I’ve felt the need to start trying to break it, to find what it can’t do. : can I use it for… everything?  → basically color the outside first?
