A 4000 Year Run

I've read quite a bit in my life. More than most. That's actually not hard as reading follows a long-tail distribution. I'm sure of it. Even though I never checked it. That's what reading does for you.

But my attitude towards reading has changed, recently. And it's all because of AI.

Reading used to be a pleasure, sure. It was also inherently frustrating: it could never be completed. I could never read all the books. (But that's stupid and I shouldn't aim for it anyways.) However, I could never read all the best books either. They just keep making them. I couldn't catch up. Ever. I would never complete "the Canon".

That has changed now.

Now I can read all the best verifiably-human written best books, because those started back in 2100 BCE and ended in 2022 CE, with the release of ChatGPT. For everything written after that date… you can never be sure.

So now it is possible, you can finish it. This has motivated me to try to make my way back, all the way to the work that has influenced the most other works and, thus, that "unlocks" the largest number of them.

All the way back to the beginning.
