#27

Imaginal Amputees

The bottleneck is gone

This is the state most of us were into as children:

We had tremendously lively imaginations, but lacked the skills to execute on them.

Part of “growing up” was to either accept the fact that our skill would never reach our imagination and to carry that cross with us or to cut our imagination down to the size of our skill, and live as imaginal amputees.

This is most unfortunate because — look around. Like, physically, literally, look around. In all likelihood everything, each single thing you see around you was an idea first.

I’m typing this on my computer, next to it I have a bottle of water, both sit on a wooden table, atop a carpet, atop a marble floor, next to a glass window and so on, and so on.

In fact, you could think of the entire evolution of human technology as us getting better and better at immanentizing our ideas.

For the first time in human history our imagination is not the bottleneck anymore.
