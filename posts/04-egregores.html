Egregores

(Although this post is not strictly about GenAI it’s about me using it to lead people to a referent)

Egregores are all the rage.

Google Trends March 2026.

“Egregore” is just a cool word for 'group-mind'.

Perhaps you’ve experienced it — it’s what you feel when you’re ‘vibing’ with someone, or with a group. Or in large-scale religious experiences. But if not, and you want a taste, read on.

"The Mind" is one of my favorite games. It’s a card game with only two rules: each round, players get a hand of cards drawn from a shared deck numbered 1–100 — one card in round 1, two in round 2, and so on. And the goal is to play all cards to a central pile in ascending order.

It's a fully cooperative game everyone wins or everyone loses.

Oh, shoot, I forgot, three rules: no talking.

Together these constraints — fully cooperative and no talking — create something.

As people try to communicate without talking, after a few rounds, players just... sync up.

It’s hard to explain. But why explain it when you can experience it?
